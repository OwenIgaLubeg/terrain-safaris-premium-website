The Ultimate Guide to Gorilla Trekking in Uganda

Uganda is home to nearly half of the world’s remaining mountain gorillas gentle giants that live deep within the misty forests of Bwindi Impenetrable National Park and Mgahinga Gorilla National Park. A gorilla trekking safari is more than a wildlife encounter; it’s a life-changing journey into one of Earth’s last wild frontiers.

Why Trek Gorillas in Uganda?

Unlike Rwanda or DR Congo, Uganda offers lower permit costs (USD $800 vs. $1,500) and access to 20+ habituated gorilla families** across four sectors of Bwindi alone. You’ll trek through lush montane forest, guided by expert trackers who know every trail and vocalization.

Each permit allows one hour with a gorilla family—close enough to see their expressive eyes, hear chest-beats, and witness playful juveniles tumble through the undergrowth.

Top Gorilla Trekking Destinations
  • Bwindi Impenetrable Forest (Southwest Uganda): UNESCO World Heritage Site with 4 trekking sectors Buhoma, Ruhija, Rushaga, and Nkuringo. Rushaga offers the only chance for gorilla habituation experience (4 hours with researchers).
  • Mgahinga Gorilla National Park: Part of the Virunga Massif, shared with Rwanda and DR Congo. Smaller but stunning, with golden monkeys and volcano hikes.

Our all inclusive gorilla safaris include:
