Bwindi

The Heart of Mountain Gorillas

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to over 450 mountain gorillas nearly half the world’s population.

Bwindi Impenetrable National Park spans 331 km² of ancient montane rainforest in southwest Uganda. Its name “impenetrable” comes from its dense network of vines, shrubs, and trees. But within this wilderness lies one of Earth’s most profound wildlife encounters: sitting face to face with a mountain gorilla family.

Highlights of Bwindi Safaris

  • Over 20 habituated mountain gorilla families.
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site with diverse flora and fauna.
  • Home to 350+ bird species, including Albertine Rift endemics.
  • Four unique trekking sectors, each with distinct experiences.
  • Option for Gorilla Habituation Experience (Spend quality time with the gorillas).
  • Cultural encounters with the indigenous Batwa pygmy communities.
  • Cool mountain climate, ideal for hiking and photography.

All Activities in Bwindi Impenetrable Forest

  • Gorilla Trekking 1 hour with a gorilla family.
  • Gorilla Habituation Experience 4 hours with researchers and gorillas.
  • Guided forest nature walks and birding tours.
  • Cultural tours with the Batwa tribe.
  • Scenic hikes through the forest trails and waterfalls.
  • Community visits supporting local tourism and crafts.
  • Photographic safaris with professional guidance.

The Four Sectors of Bwindi

Bwindi is divided into four distinct trekking sectors, each offering unique terrain, difficulty levels, and gorilla groups.

1. Buhoma (Northwest)

The original and most accessible sector. Ideal for first time trekkers. Home to the famous Mubare, Habinyanja, and Rushegura families.

  • Steepness: Gentle, relatively easy.
  • Best for: First-time trekkers, families, and older travelers.
  • Highlights: Closest to Queen Elizabeth NP; historic Mubare group.

2. Ruhija (East)

A birder’s paradise at 2,300m elevation. Trek the Oruzogo or Mukiza families through steep, misty trails.

  • Steepness: Moderate to steep.
  • Best for: Birders, nature lovers, moderately fit hikers.
  • Highlights: Stunning views, fewer crowds, rare birds.

3. Rushaga (South)

The largest sector with 8+ habituated families, including the massive Nshongi group. Offers the only Gorilla Habituation Experience.

  • Steepness: Steep and hilly.
  • Best for: Adventure seekers, short-notice bookings.
  • Highlights: Many gorilla families, Gorilla Habituation Experience.

4. Nkuringo (Southwest)

Challenging but breathtaking. Trek the Nkuringo family along steep slopes with panoramic views of the Virunga Volcanoes.

  • Steepness: Very steep; most challenging.
  • Best for: Physically fit and experienced hikers.
  • Highlights: Epic views of Virunga Volcanoes, fewer tourists.

Accommodation Options

Bwindi offers a wide range of stays, from luxurious jungle lodges to comfortable midrange camps and affordable community-run options.

Popular lodges include:

  • Luxury: Buhoma Lodge, Mahogany Springs, Sanctuary Gorilla Forest Camp
  • Midrange: Rushaga Gorilla Camp, Nkuringo Bwindi Gorilla Lodge
  • Budget/Community: Ride 4 a Woman, Bwindi View Bandas

All lodges are strategically located within Bwindi’s four main sectors, offering easy access to nearby gorilla trekking start points and breathtaking views of the surrounding rainforest.

Best Time to Visit Bwindi

  • June to September and December to February Dry seasons with the best hiking conditions.
  • Gorilla trekking is available year round, but trails may be slippery during the rainy seasons (March–May and October–November).

KEY: For the best gorilla safari in Uganda, book your permits and lodge at least 4 months in advance.

How to Get to Bwindi Impenetrable National Park

  • By road: 8–10 hours drive from Kampala or Entebbe.
  • By air: Daily domestic flights from Entebbe to Kihihi (for Buhoma) or Kisoro (for Rushaga/Nkuringo).

Terrain Safaris will arrange transfers and park entry logistics for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How do I obtain a gorilla trekking permit?

Gorilla trekking permits can be purchased through the Uganda Wildlife Authority or authorized tour operators like Terrain Safaris. We recommend booking 4–6 months in advance.

2. What should I wear for gorilla trekking?

Wear sturdy hiking boots, long-sleeved shirts, long pants, gardening gloves, and a rain jacket. Bring a small backpack with water and snacks.

3. Is gorilla trekking safe?

Yes. You’ll be accompanied by armed ranger guides and trackers. Gorillas are gentle, but always follow your guide’s instructions.

4. What wildlife can I see besides gorillas?

Bwindi hosts chimpanzees, L’Hoest’s monkeys, forest elephants, duikers, and over 350 bird species including the African Green Broadbill and Rwenzori Turaco.

5. What should I pack for Bwindi?

Essentials: passport, permit, camera, extra batteries, binoculars, sunscreen, hat, insect repellent, waterproof bag, and cash for tips.
