Mgahinga

Where Gold Meets Silver

Track gorillas, golden monkeys, and hike the Virunga volcanoes in Uganda’s most magical park.

“Where Gold Meets Silver” Nestled in Uganda’s southwestern corner, Mgahinga Gorilla National Park is a hidden gem in the Virunga Mountains. Though Uganda’s smallest national park (just 33.7 km²), it offers a rare dual primate experience: tracking mountain gorillas and the endangered golden monkey in one place. Part of the transboundary Virunga Conservation Area (shared with Rwanda and DRC), Mgahinga is ideal for travelers seeking exclusive, crowd free gorilla safaris, volcano adventures, and authentic Batwa cultural encounters.

Highlights of Mgahinga Gorilla National Park

  • Home to the Nyakagezi gorilla family, the only habituated group in the park.
  • One of the few places in the world to spot the golden monkey.
  • Spectacular hikes on the Virunga Volcanoes – Mt. Gahinga, Mt. Muhabura & Mt. Sabyinyo.
  • Rich Batwa cultural encounters with indigenous forest-dwelling communities.
  • Ideal for exclusive, less-crowded gorilla safaris in Uganda.

Top Activities in Mgahinga Gorilla National Park

  • Gorilla Trekking Track the Nyakagezi family in the bamboo forests.
  • Golden Monkey Tracking Unique and lively experience in the high altitude forest.
  • Volcano Hiking Summits of Mt. Sabyinyo (3,669m), Mt. Gahinga (3,474m), and Mt. Muhabura (4,127m).
  • Batwa Cultural Trail Explore the forest from the perspective of its first people.
  • Bird Watching Spot over 180 bird species including the Rwenzori turaco.
  • Photography Safaris Incredible light, wildlife, and unspoiled scenery ideal for professional photographers.

Accommodation in Mgahinga Gorilla National Park

All accommodations are within easy reach of the park headquarters in Ntebeko and provide access to various activities in and around Mgahinga.

Popular accommodation facilities include:

  • Luxury: Mount Gahinga Lodge an exclusive eco-luxury lodge with volcano views.
  • Midrange: Lake Chahafi Resort, Travellers Rest Hotel in Kisoro.
  • Budget: Amajambere Iwacu Community Camp simple, community run accommodation near the park gate.

Best Time to Visit Mgahinga Gorilla National Park

The best time to visit Mgahinga is during the dry seasons, from June to September and December to February, when trekking conditions are optimal. However, gorilla trekking is possible year round.

How to Get to Mgahinga Gorilla National Park

  • By Road: Approximately 9–10 hours’ drive from Kampala or 4 hours from Lake Mburo National Park.
  • By Air: Scheduled or chartered flights from Entebbe to Kisoro Airstrip (1 hour), then a 1-hour drive to the park.

Terrain Safaris will arrange transfers and park entry logistics for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. How long does gorilla trekking in Mgahinga take?

The duration can vary from 2 hours to three-quarters of the day, depending on the gorillas' location.

2. How physically demanding is gorilla trekking?

Trekking can be strenuous, involving hiking through steep and sometimes challenging terrain, so a reasonable level of fitness is recommended.

3. What wildlife can I see besides gorillas?

The park is home to golden monkeys, over 180 bird species (including the Rwenzori turaco), duikers, bushbucks, and unique Afro-montane flora.

4. Is it safe to visit Mgahinga Gorilla National Park?

Yes, the park is generally safe for tourists. You’ll be accompanied by armed ranger guides at all times.

5. How do I obtain a gorilla trekking permit?

Permits can be purchased through the Uganda Wildlife Authority or authorized tour operators like Terrain Safaris. Book 3–6 months in advance.
